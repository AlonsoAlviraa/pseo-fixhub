Error Code IE

Fixing LG Error IE

Official troubleshooting guide for LG Dishwasher (QuadWash Series)

Diagnostic Summary

Severity Low
Est. Cost $20 - $50
Difficulty Easy (DIY-friendly)
Time Required 48-68 minutes
Safety First: Disconnect power and water supplies before opening panels. Keep floors dry to prevent slips.

What This Error Looks Like

Likely Symptoms

  • Intermittent performance or repeated code IE after a reset
  • Cycles stopping unexpectedly before completion
  • Unusual noises or vibrations during operation
  • Visible debris or lint accumulation around service panels
  • Moisture around hose connections or kinks along the line
  • Persistent odor from standing water between washes

Common Root Causes

  • Temporary glitch cleared by a full power cycle
  • Blocked air or water pathways reducing flow efficiency
  • Loose connectors or aging components that need reseating
  • Maintenance overdue on intake or debris filters

Tools & Prep Checklist

Diagnostic Checks

  • Power-cycle the appliance for 5 minutes to clear transient faults.
  • Verify the unit is level and stable to reduce vibration-related codes.
  • Run a short cycle and listen for the drain pump; note any unusual noises.
  • Inspect filters for debris under running water until flow is clear.

Tools

  • Phillips and flathead screwdrivers
  • Microfiber towels for cleanup
  • Smartphone flashlight or headlamp
  • Bucket or shallow pan for capturing residual water
  • Needle-nose pliers for clamps and hoses
  • Replacement parts specific to your dishwasher model

Detailed Walkthrough

Step 1

Check the water inlet valve. Take a moment to confirm the step is completed before moving forward to avoid repeating diagnostics.

Step 2

Ensure water supply is turned on. Take a moment to confirm the step is completed before moving forward to avoid repeating diagnostics.

Step 3

Inspect the inlet hose for kinks. Clearing hoses and drain paths restores proper water flow and removes the most common cause of this code.

Step 4

Clean the inlet filter screen. A clogged filter restricts circulation; rinsing it under running water often clears the code immediately.

Keep the Error From Coming Back

  • Log the date of this repair so you can identify recurring patterns over time.
  • Run a quick rinse or empty cycle monthly to keep sensors clean.
  • Inspect power cords and hoses for visible wear whenever you move the appliance.
  • Add a calendar reminder to clean intake and drain filters every 30 days.
Need help? If the error returns after completing these steps twice, contact a certified technician to check the control board, pump, and wiring harness.
